Jube is open source under AGPLv3, with no per-client licence fee. For system integrators
and technology partners, this creates a genuine opportunity to deliver a modern, credible
AML and fraud detection capability to clients who need transparency, control, and regulatory
defensibility — without the commercial friction of proprietary licensing.

The Provider operates in the part of this value chain where it adds most value: architecture,
development, and implementation of the Jube platform. Partners hold the client relationship,
deliver the service wrapper, and build their own IP on top of Jube's extensibility framework.
That alignment is deliberate and worth something. The Provider has no commercial interest in
operating outside that position, and no intention of using any operational contact with end
clients as a mechanism to displace or circumvent a partner's role. Partners can engage with
confidence that the Provider is a committed participant in the value chain, not a threat to it.

**What we offer**

- **Training and certification** — the prerequisite for any partner engagement. Your team
  goes through the same Training and Integration programme as end clients, emerging with the
  skills and certification to deliver Jube independently. Certification is the gateway to all
  subsequent partner arrangements, including co-delivery and Expert of Record.
- **Expert of Record** — the Provider is available as named technical authority on
  partner-led engagements, supporting governance, audit, and regulatory documentation
  requirements. Expert of Record designation is contingent on infrastructure and Runbook
  conditions being met, as set out in the JTOS.
- **Co-delivery** — available for complex or heavily regulated implementations where the
  Provider participates as a specialist technical contributor alongside the partner's team.
  Co-delivery requires a separate Work Order and is only available where the Provider assumes
  the Expert of Record role for the full duration.
- **PAYG and Enterprise Support** — ongoing post-live support arrangements are available
  to certified end clients directly or through a partner structure, on the same published
  terms regardless of channel.

**Who this is for**

System integrators, technology consultancies, and regional delivery partners serving banks,
fintechs, and payment institutions in emerging and growth markets.

**Pricing is published and fixed.** Training, implementation, and support fees are set out
on this site and do not vary by channel. End clients can and should verify what the Jube
component of any engagement costs directly. Where discounts have been agreed as part of a
specific commercial arrangement, those remain confidential between the parties in accordance
with the JTOS.

**How the terms work**

All engagements — whether direct or through a partner — are delivered under the
[Jube Terms of Service (JTOS)](/agreements/jtos-version-1-17), which is published, versioned,
and non-negotiable. Where a partner operates their own terms above or alongside the JTOS,
those terms do not modify, supersede, or vary the JTOS as between the Provider and the named
end client. Partners are expected to represent this accurately to end clients and to inform
end clients of their obligations under the AGPLv3 licence prior to deployment.

**Getting started**

The natural first step is putting your team through the
[Jube Training and Integration programme](/jube-training). Certification and a partner
relationship follow naturally from there.
